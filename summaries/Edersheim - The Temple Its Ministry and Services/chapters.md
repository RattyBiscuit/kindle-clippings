### Chapter 1: A First View of Jerusalem, and of the Temple

Edersheim introduces the geographical context of Jerusalem and the Temple itself, detailing its grandeur and significance as a place of worship and spiritual centrality for the Jewish people.

### Chapter 2: Within the Holy Place

This chapter describes the Holy Place, its furnishings, and the responsibilities of the priests. Edersheim details the conception of holiness that surrounded the space.

### Chapter 3: Temple Order, Revenues, and Music

The chapter discusses the operational structure of the Temple, focusing on its financial management and the musical traditions that accompanied worship.

### Chapter 4: The Officiating Priesthood

Here, Edersheim outlines the roles of the priests, including their training, duties, and the symbolic nature of their practices. He examines the makeup of the priestly family and their significance.

### Chapter 5: Sacrifices: Their Order and Their Meaning

This chapter delves into the various types of offerings made at the Temple, elucidating their spiritual symbolism and the rituals associated with each.

### Chapter 6: The Burnt-Offering, the Sin- and Trespass-Offering, and the Peace-Offering

Edersheim provides a comprehensive look at these specific sacrifices, explaining their purposes and the theological implications behind them.

### Chapter 7: At Night in the Temple

This chapter describes the activities that took place in the Temple during the night, illuminating the ever-watchful presence of the priests and the ongoing nature of worship.

### Chapter 8: The Morning and the Evening Sacrifice

Edersheim examines these crucial sacrifices, detailing their significance and the rituals surrounding their performance.

### Chapter 9: Sabbath in the Temple

He discusses how the Sabbath was observed within the Temple setting, including rituals and practices that distinguish it from other days.

### Chapter 10: Festive Cycles and Arrangement of the Calendar

This chapter reviews the Jewish calendar and its connection to Temple celebrations, detailing the rhythms of worship throughout the year.

### Chapter 11: The Passover

Edersheim elaborates on the Passover festival, its rituals, and its importance in the national and religious identity of the Jewish people.

### Chapter 12: The Paschal Feast and the Lord's Supper

This chapter draws parallels between the Paschal feast and the Lord's Supper, exploring their theological implications within the context of Christianity.

### Chapter 13: The Feast of Unleavened Bread and the Day of Pentecost

Edersheim outlines these key feasts, emphasizing their significance in Jewish tradition and their connections to biblical events.

### Chapter 14: The Feast of Tabernacles

This chapter details the rituals associated with this harvest festival and its meaning in the life of the Jewish people.

### Chapter 15: The New Moons

Edersheim discusses the observance of the new moons and how they fit into the broader context of Jewish worship and calendar.

### Chapter 16: The Day of Atonement

He elaborates on the unique observances of this solemn day, focusing on the rituals of atonement and repentance.

### Chapter 17: Post-Mosaic Festivals

This chapter goes into the festivals established after the Mosaic law, exploring their significance and how they functioned within worship.

### Chapter 18: On Purifications

Edersheim examines the rites of purification, including the cleansing rituals in various contexts, from those for the sick to those for the community.

### Chapter 19: On Vows

The final chapter covers the concept of vows in Jewish practice and their implications for personal and communal faith.

---

Edersheim's work is a valuable resource for understanding the Temple's complex ritual life and its indispensable role in shaping the religious landscape of early Christianity. Each chapter offers a glimpse into the intricate relationship between the Temple's services and the faith of those who worshipped there, framing it as a vital context for New Testament events.
