### Introduction: Welcome to the Biblical Cosmos

Parry introduces the readers to the concept of a biblical cosmos, highlighting that the ancient worldview was vastly different from contemporary perspectives.

### Part I: A Tour of the Biblical Earth

#### Chapter 1: Joining the Flat Earth Society: The Big Picture

The author examines the flat earth conceptualisation of the ancient Israelites, contrasting it with modern scientific understandings.

#### Chapter 2: Here Be Dragons! The Sea

This chapter focuses on the sea as a symbol of chaos and danger in the biblical narrative, touching upon cultural meanings of water imagery.

#### Chapter 3: Between the Devil and the Deep Blue Sea: Land

Parry discusses the importance of land in biblical texts as a space for divine encounters and human activity, signifying God's presence.

#### Chapter 4: A Land Down Under: Sheol/Hades

The chapter covers the notion of Sheol as an underworld realm, elaborating on its significance in biblical thought.

### Part II: A Tour of the Biblical Heavens

#### Chapter 5: Eyes in Their Stars: The Sky

Parry explores how ancient people viewed the stars as divine beings and the implications of this belief on their understanding of the cosmos.

#### Chapter 6: Brighter than a Thousand Suns: God's Heaven

This section delves into the depiction of God’s heaven, highlighting its role in the biblical narrative and its connection to God's authority over creation.

### Part III: The House of God: Temple and Cosmos

#### Chapter 7: God's in the House: The Temple and the Biblical Cosmos

Parry draws parallels between the structure of the temple and the cosmos, suggesting that the two are understood more cohesively in ancient thought.

#### Chapter 8: Christ's in the House: Jesus and the Biblical Cosmos

This chapter integrates the life of Jesus within the framework of the biblical cosmos, showing his connection to both heaven and earth.

### Part IV: Can We Inhabit the Biblical Cosmos?

#### Chapter 9: How Can We Inhabit the Biblical Cosmos Today?

Parry discusses how modern readers can connect with the ancient cosmology while maintaining faith.

#### Chapter 10: The Cosmic Temple Today

This chapter examines the implications of the temple metaphor in understanding God’s presence in the modern world.

#### Chapter 11: The Biblical Heavens Today

Parry reflects on the continuing relevance of the biblical conception of heaven in contemporary spirituality.

#### Chapter 12: The Biblical Earth Today

Concluding the book, Parry encourages readers to engage with the biblical understanding of earth in a way that enhances their spiritual journey.

### Bibliography

The book concludes with a bibliography for further reading, providing resources to deepen understanding of biblical cosmology.

This comprehensive analysis illustrates the depth and uniqueness of Robin Parry's examination of the biblical worldview. By delving into the biblical cosmos, Parry invites readers to rethink their theological perspectives in light of ancient understanding.
